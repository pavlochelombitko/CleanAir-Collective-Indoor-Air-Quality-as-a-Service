The Problem

Indoor air is silently harming millions of people every day

We spend up to 90% of our time indoors, yet most buildings have zero visibility into air quality. Poor indoor air causes headaches, fatigue, reduced focus, and long-term health damage — costing businesses billions.

🧠

Cognitive Decline

Harvard studies show that high CO₂ levels reduce cognitive function by up to 50%. Workers in poorly ventilated offices make worse decisions and are less productive.

-50%Cognitive performance at 1,400 ppm CO₂
🏫

Sick Building Syndrome

Schools and offices with poor ventilation see higher rates of absenteeism, respiratory issues, and allergies. Children are especially vulnerable to airborne pollutants.

3.5MSchool days lost in EU to poor air yearly
💶

Hidden Economic Costs

Poor IAQ costs European employers an estimated €20B annually in lost productivity, sick days, and healthcare. Yet most companies don't even measure it.

€20BAnnual productivity loss in the EU
⚡

Energy Waste

Buildings often over-ventilate or under-ventilate because they lack data. Smart air quality monitoring enables demand-controlled ventilation, cutting HVAC energy use by 20-30%.

30%Potential HVAC energy savings
